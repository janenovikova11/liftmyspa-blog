# How to Get More 5-Star Reviews for Your Med Spa

## A direct system for turning happy clients into a steady stream of public five-star reviews

Reviews are the most underused growth lever in most med spas. They drive Google ranking, ad conversion rates, and consult close rates. Yet the typical med spa collects them by accident. With an automated request system, Lift My Spa clients see up to 60% more 5-star reviews. Here is how to build the system.

## Why med spas underperform on reviews

The pattern is consistent.

- Staff are supposed to ask but rarely do
- The ask is verbal and forgettable
- Clients are sent home and never reminded
- The review link is hard to find on the phone
- Spanish-speaking clients are not asked in Spanish
- Unhappy clients post in public; happy clients say nothing
- One-time asks, no follow-up

Result: a clinic with hundreds of delighted clients shows up online with twenty reviews and a 4.3 rating.

## Step 1: Ask every happy client

The single biggest unlock is this. Ask everyone, every time. Not the regulars. Not the easy ones. Everyone who walks out happy.

Manual asking does not scale. The Lift My Spa automated review request system triggers a request after every successful appointment, in the right channel, at the right moment.

## Step 2: Time the ask correctly

Wrong timing produces silence. The right timing.

- For consults that converted to a booking, ask after the booked treatment
- For Botox and tox treatments, ask 7 to 10 days after, when results are visible
- For laser treatments, ask after the result window for that treatment
- For events and packages, ask within 24 hours of the visit
- For membership renewals, ask after the second visit of the renewal period

Asking the moment the client walks out of a treatment they cannot yet evaluate produces weak reviews. Asking when results show produces strong ones.

## Step 3: Send the ask in the right channel

SMS open rates run dramatically higher than email. Most reviews come from SMS. Email is a useful backup. Lift My Spa sends through both.

## Step 4: Make the link one tap

The review link should drop the client directly into the Google review form, pre-filled where possible. Any extra step kills conversion.

## Step 5: Use a smart filter for unhappy clients

Not every client should be funneled to a public review. The right system asks for satisfaction first. Happy clients go to Google. Unhappy clients go to a private feedback path so the practice can address the issue and prevent a public negative review.

This is not gaming the system. This is good service. Lift My Spa runs this filter automatically.

## Step 6: Follow up if no response

Most reviews come from the second or third nudge, not the first. The right cadence.

- Initial request shortly after the result window
- Friendly nudge 48 to 72 hours later if no review
- Final ask a week later, if appropriate

Manual follow-up is where review programs die. Automated follow-up is where they thrive.

## Step 7: Ask in Spanish when appropriate

In Texas, Florida, and Arizona markets, a meaningful share of clients prefer Spanish. The Lift My Spa system asks in the client's preferred language so the review actually gets written.

## Step 8: Connect reviews to referrals

A client who just left a five-star review is the highest probability referrer in the practice. Trigger the referral ask immediately after the review posts. Reviews and referrals reinforce each other.

## Step 9: Respond to every review

Public responses to both positive and negative reviews matter for ranking and trust. The right response is fast, professional, and personal. Negative reviews need acknowledgment and, when appropriate, an offline resolution path.

## Step 10: Track the metrics that matter

In the Lift My Spa ROI dashboard, monitor.

- Reviews requested per week
- Reviews posted per week
- Five-star rate
- Average rating trend
- Channel mix (SMS vs email)
- Spanish vs English review volume
- Time from treatment to review

If five-star rate is below the upper 80s percentage range, the result-window timing or the satisfaction filter probably need adjusting.

## Why reviews drive revenue, not just brand

Reviews change three things directly.

- Local Google ranking, which drives more "med spa near me" calls
- Google Ads conversion rate, which lowers cost per booked consult
- Consult close rate, because new clients come in already trusting

A med spa that goes from 50 reviews and 4.3 stars to 300 reviews and 4.8 stars sees real movement on all three. That movement compounds.
