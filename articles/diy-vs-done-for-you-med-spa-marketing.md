# DIY vs Done-For-You Med Spa Marketing: Which to Pick

## A clear framework for choosing the right operating model based on your team, your stage, and your goals

Most med spa owners think the choice is binary. Run marketing yourself or hand it off entirely. It is not. The right model depends on internal capacity, growth stage, and how much of the platform you want to operate. This guide walks through the framework, with the trade-offs spelled out.

## The three real options

Pure DIY, pure done-for-you, and the hybrid in the middle. All three have a place. The wrong choice burns money. The right one accelerates growth.

### Option 1: DIY

You run the platform. Your team handles the workflows, the messaging, the landing pages, the ad campaigns, the review requests, the referrals.

Best for.
- Practices with an in-house marketer who knows med spa specifics
- Owners who want full control and visibility
- Multi-location groups with internal marketing operations

Worst for.
- Owners trying to do this on top of clinical work
- Practices without dedicated marketing capacity
- Teams who will not actually run the workflows

### Option 2: Assisted (hybrid)

A platform plus a partner. Your team runs day-to-day operations. The partner handles strategy, optimization, and the heavier campaign work.

Best for.
- Owners who want guidance without giving up control
- Practices with limited marketing capacity but real growth ambition
- Owners stepping up from DIY without going all the way to outsourced

Worst for.
- Owners who do not want to look at the platform at all
- Owners who want zero involvement

### Option 3: Done-for-you

The partner runs the entire system on your behalf. You see the results in the dashboard.

Best for.
- Owners focused on clinical work and operations
- Practices without internal marketing capacity
- Owners who value time over control
- Multi-location groups standardizing across sites

Worst for.
- Owners who insist on touching every detail
- Practices on the smallest budgets

## How to decide: the four diagnostic questions

### 1. Do you have someone in-house who can actually run this?

"Run this" means writing copy, building campaigns, optimizing ads, monitoring workflows, responding to issues, and reporting weekly. Not "the front desk lead has time on Tuesdays". Honest answer matters.

If yes, DIY is on the table. If no, assisted or done-for-you.

### 2. How much time can the owner realistically give to marketing?

Med spa owners who try to run marketing alongside injecting and managing the practice usually under-invest in both. If the realistic time budget is under five hours a week, do not pick DIY.

### 3. How urgent is growth?

A practice that needs to grow fast cannot afford the learning curve of DIY. The ramp-up time is months. Assisted or done-for-you produce results faster because expertise is already in the system.

### 4. What is the cost of slow?

In a market like Maricopa, where med spa search demand is up 173% year over year, every quarter spent learning is a quarter competitors spend booking. The cost of slow is real.

## Where each option breaks

### DIY breaks when

- The marketer leaves and the practice has no continuity
- Workflows go untouched for weeks
- Ad spend keeps running while no one is optimizing
- The owner ends up doing it on weekends
- Reviews and referrals get forgotten

### Done-for-you breaks when

- The owner has no visibility into what is being done
- Reporting is vanity metrics, not booked revenue
- The partner has no med spa specialization
- The contract is long-term and hard to exit

### Assisted breaks when

- Roles between owner and partner are unclear
- Communication cadence is too thin
- The partner is reactive, not proactive

The right setup mitigates each of these failure modes.

## What to look for regardless of which option you pick

These are non-negotiable for any med spa.

- Built only for med spas. Generalist platforms cost months of customization.
- 24/7 phone coverage. Over 50% of med spa calls go unanswered industry-wide.
- Bilingual English and Spanish, especially in Texas, Florida, and Arizona.
- HIPAA-aware data and messaging.
- Transparent pricing. No long-term contracts.
- Revenue-focused reporting, not impressions.
- Live in two weeks, not three to six months.
