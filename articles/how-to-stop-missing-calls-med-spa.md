# How to Stop Missing Calls at Your Med Spa

## A direct guide to capturing every inbound call without hiring more receptionists

Over 50% of med spa calls go unanswered. Industry-wide, 97.6% of healthcare bookings still happen by phone. That math means most med spas are losing more than half of their highest-intent buyers before a real conversation ever begins. The fix is straightforward. Replace voicemail with an AI front desk that answers every call, books on the spot, and never sleeps.

## Why med spas miss so many calls

The reasons are predictable.

- The front desk is with a client, performing intake, taking payment, or restocking.
- The injector is mid-treatment and cannot pick up.
- It is after hours, evenings, or weekends, when prospects actually call.
- A second line rings while the first is in use.
- Lunch breaks, sick days, and turnover create coverage gaps.
- Spanish-speaking callers reach an English-only voicemail and hang up.

Each missed call is a high-intent buyer in a high-ticket industry, gone to the next clinic on Google.

## What a missed call actually costs

Run the numbers for one location. Average med spa consult value sits in the hundreds. Conversion to package or membership stretches that into thousands per booked client. If you miss ten calls a week and even one of those would have booked a tox or laser package, you are losing thousands monthly. Stack that over a year and the missed call problem is the largest revenue leak in the practice.

## Step 1: Stop relying on voicemail

Voicemail is a dead channel. Most med spa prospects will not leave a message. They will call the next clinic in the search results. Voicemail is not a backup. It is a leak.

## Step 2: Stop trying to fix this with more humans

Hiring a second or third receptionist looks like the obvious answer. It is not.

- Payroll, taxes, training, and benefits add up fast.
- Humans take lunch, sleep, get sick, and turn over.
- Even a fully staffed front desk does not cover 8pm or Sunday calls.
- Bilingual coverage is hard to staff for and harder to retain.

You can fully staff the desk and still miss the calls that matter most.
