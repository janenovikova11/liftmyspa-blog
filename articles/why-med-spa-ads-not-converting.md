# The Real Reason Your Med Spa Ads Aren't Converting

Most med spa owners blame the ad when conversions disappoint. The headline, the creative, the targeting. Sometimes that is the issue. Most of the time, the ad is fine. The funnel behind it is what is leaking.

## Problem Overview

A typical med spa ad campaign goes through a familiar arc. The agency or in-house marketer launches a Meta or Google ad. The clicks come in. The cost per click is reasonable. The leads start arriving. Then the booked appointment count comes back, and it is a fraction of what was expected.

The first instinct is to blame the creative. The second instinct is to blame the targeting. The third is to ask for a new landing page. None of those are usually the real problem, because none of those are where the leak actually lives.

The leak almost always lives between the moment the lead is captured and the moment they are supposed to book. That is the funnel. And in most med spas, the funnel is held together by sticky notes, inboxes, and good intentions.

Here is what typically happens to a paid lead.

The lead fills out a form at 8:47 PM, on their couch, while watching TV. The form lands in an inbox. Nobody is watching the inbox at 8:47 PM. The lead does not get a response that night. The next morning, the front desk is slammed with check-ins. The lead is finally called at 11 AM, almost 14 hours later. By that time the lead has either forgotten, booked elsewhere, or stopped caring. The call goes to voicemail. The voicemail is not returned. The lead is gone.

The ad was fine. The targeting was fine. The funnel was broken.

## Expert Insight

The conversion gap in med spa advertising is structural, and it has three layers.

The first layer is response speed. Lead response data shows that responding within 5 minutes converts dramatically more often than responding after 30 minutes. Most paid leads in the med spa industry are not getting a response inside that window. The campaign is essentially paying to deliver leads to a black hole.

The second layer is multi-touch nurture. Even when a lead is responded to quickly, a single response is not enough. Clients researching Botox, filler, laser, or memberships often need 3 to 7 touches across SMS and email before they book. Practices that send one email and stop are paying for leads they never finish converting.

The third layer is multi-channel routing. Leads do not all come in through the same channel. Some come from web forms, some from Instagram DMs, some from Google ads call extensions, some from Facebook lead forms, some from TikTok. Each channel has its own response surface. Without a unified system, the response time, quality, and follow-up vary wildly by channel, and most practices have no idea which channels are leaking.

Ad spend audited against booked revenue almost always tells the same story. The cost per lead looks reasonable. The cost per booked appointment is brutal. The gap between those two numbers is the funnel leak. And no amount of creative testing will close it, because the issue is not at the top of the funnel.

The fix is to engineer the funnel itself. Instant response on every channel, structured nurture across SMS and email, and a unified system that ties the ad spend back to the booked appointment so the leaks become visible.
